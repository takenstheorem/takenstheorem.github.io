Abundance (by ZeroContract Artist Studio)
MATERIALS OF ABUNDANCE on objtk.com

Last year, in summer 2024, I worked with Ernesto Asch on RAW MATERIALS. The challenge was to use the artist as a source of data for new visuals. I extracted the millions of pixels from his works, and rendered new, curious forms. Artists into their raw materials. Mind pixels.

ZeroContract presents a new challenge with its inaugural "Abundance" series. There were 28 contributions from distinct artists. I extracted their millions of pixels, and fused them in pairs into 14 new forms. I chose forms expressing the idea of abundance. Using the pixels of each pair of pieces, I reconstituted images of the awe-inspiring abundance of nature, industry, and the sometimes maladaptive consequences of their combination.

I tried to stay true to the original works, fusing them meaningfully under the selected images. This was challenging sometimes. Artists in "Abundance" sometimes used strikingly simple palettes, other times more opulent and complex. When fusing them, I tried to use these differences as a resource, sometimes in coherence, sometimes as contrast. Some reconstitutions have insufficient raw material, they have bands of color that can indicate absence -- or saturation. I hope you find them intriguing or even beautiful.

The pieces below are ordered from nature to industry to psychology. Almost like a natural evolution of a mass universe whose abundance cannot be fully fathomed but sometimes felt.

Reconstituted base images from Library of Congress 'free to use' and Wikimedia's public domain images. I do not link to them. The viewer can interpret the reconstituted images through their own lens. Each piece is a 1,000 x 1,000 JPG. The JPG has the form of these public domain images of 'abundance,' but their pixels derive from the pixels of two "Abundance" pieces.
